Postdoc at CBMN, CNRS

This works builds on my postdoc at INRAE. The goal was to analyse jointly immunofluorescence microscopy and mass spectrometry images, as well as MR and MS images.


Fusion of IF/MRI and MALDI images

We use and propose carefully selected statistical methods for the joint analysis of MS images. Learn more

This work focuses mainly on statistical methods to identify ions in MSI that are specific of cellular regions in IF images. We use sequential ROC analyses, t-SNE, Kendrick-Mass defects representations... We also propose a new method to identify noisy ion images. In another work, we study the mouse brain through MR and MS images. One of the main challenges is the deformations that are induced during sample cutting. Traditional registration methods use similarity metrics based on image intensities. MALDI-MS and MR images have different intensity distributions that cannot be accounted for by traditional similarity metrics. We propose a novel similarity metric for deformable registration, based on the update of distance transformation values. This work was published in the MIUA 2023 conference.

Postdoc at INRAE

The objective of this work is to search for the chemical structures of cell wall molecules which co-localize with water in wheat grains.


Fusion of MRI and MALDI images

We propose a new workflow which merge the information from MRI and MALDI images. Learn more

Our workflow involves several image processing steps. First, the shape of the wheat grain is extracted on both images. Secondly, the segmented grain in the MALDI image is registered onto the segmented grain in the MRI image. An initial rigid registration method is used, and allows for a global alignment of the grains. However, it does not compensate for the local geometrical differences. Thus, the first rigid registration is followed by the non-rigid registration method of Fischer et Modersitzki. Thirdly, we find correlations between registered ion images and the water distribution. This is achieved by statistical analysis on the images, using dimension reduction methods.

A new data structure for efficient search on isovists

In this work, we proposed a new data structure allowing to perform efficient spatial queries on unbounded isovists. It builds a R-tree from the projection of isovists in a dual space (paper submitted). The isovist computation is based on a algorithm by Suleiman.

Cross-section estimation on tubular volumes

Obtaining a precise cross-section is the critical step to perform quantitative analysis of tubular organs, for which diameter or area are often correlated to pathologies. Learn more

Orthogonal plane computation on a tubular volume consists in computing the 3D tangent on a discrete curve (the volume's skeleton). Various techniques exist for this purpose, but most of them are sensitive to irregularities in the curve. Moreover, they are dependent on the skeletonization algorithm. Using Voronoï Covariance Measure (VCM), we compute orthogonal planes which are robust to irregularities and noise. The idea is that the Voronoï cells are elongated in two directions (different shades of gray, see left picture): those of the basis of the orthogonal plane. Interestingly, since the VCM can be computed on any compact, it is not necessary to compute the skeleton as a first step, and orthogonal planes can be computed directly from the volume.

Centerlines of tubular volumes based on orthogonal plane estimation

The curve-skeleton is central to many applications in discrete geometry, since it captures interesting geometrical and topological properties with respect to the initial volume. Learn more

Since orthogonal plane computation can be done on a volume, we designed a curve-skeleton algorithm. A skeleton point corresponds to a center of mass of the intersection between an orthogonal plane and the volume. The orthogonal plane also allows to track the next point (the plane normal gives this information). The rest of the algorithm is dedicated to computation efficiency, junction and end-point detection.
